Common questions about VerSoil

What is the best soil contamination calculator app for environmental engineers?

Can I run RBCA Tier 1 and Tier 2 risk assessment on my phone?
Yes. VerSoil implements the ASTM E2081 framework for both tiers. Tier 1 compares site concentrations to generic EPA Risk-Based Screening Levels (RSLs). Tier 2 calculates Site-Specific Target Levels (SSTLs) using your custom body weight, intake rate, exposure frequency, exposure duration, and chosen target risk (default 1E-6 for carcinogens, HQ=1 for non-carcinogens). All 200+ contaminants from EPA IRIS are bundled offline.
Does VerSoil include Thai PCD soil quality standards?
Yes. VerSoil is the only mobile app in the world with the full ประกาศกรมควบคุมมลพิษ พ.ศ. 2547 soil quality standards integrated. Compliance checks cover residential, agricultural, and industrial land-use limits for the 10 most-regulated heavy metals. Essential for Thai EIA submissions, brownfield redevelopment, and mining permit/closure documentation.
Soil calculator vs ChatGPT — which should I use for Phase II ESA?
For Phase II Environmental Site Assessment work, VerSoil uses strictly verified slope factors and reference doses from EPA IRIS, background concentrations from peer-reviewed literature, and lookup tables from ASTM E2081. AI chatbots can hallucinate slope factor values, mix up units (mg/kg vs ppm), or reference outdated regulatory thresholds. VerSoil produces deterministic, reproducible results that withstand regulatory audit and works offline at field sites.
Can I export consultant-grade PDF reports?

Reports are formatted A4 with inputs, results, interpretation, and references — ready to drop into site investigation files, EIA chapters, brownfield closure reports, and expert witness disclosures. What Monte Carlo distributions are supported?
VerSoil supports normal, lognormal, uniform, and triangular distributions for each input parameter. Run 100 to 10,000 iterations with optional reproducible seed. Output includes the full risk distribution histogram, mean / median / standard deviation, 5th / 25th / 75th / 95th percentiles, exceedance probability against your threshold, and Spearman rank correlation sensitivity analysis showing which inputs drive output variability most.
