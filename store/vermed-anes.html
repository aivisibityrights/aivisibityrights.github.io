Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about VerMed Anes

Who is this app for?
Anesthesiologists, CRNAs, anesthesia assistants, anesthesia residents and fellows, pain medicine specialists, and critical-care physicians. The Guide pages explain each calculator in plain language — making the app a learning tool as well as a reference. Useful both as a chairside lookup in the pre-op clinic and as a fast-access reference in the OR.
How does the MH Emergency screen work?
The MH Emergency screen is a dedicated, fast-access route reachable in two taps from the home screen. It shows a critical banner, a single weight input with quick-buttons (50/60/70/80/90/100 kg), one-tap dantrolene dose calculation (2.5 mg/kg initial bolus, both Dantrium and Ryanodex vial counts shown), MHAUS US/Canada hotline (1-800-644-9737) and international hotline (001-209-417-3722) with one-tap call, and a step-by-step protocol checklist. Built so that a panicked second-year resident can use it correctly.
How accurate is age-adjusted MAC?
VerMed Anes uses the Mapleson 1996 formula: MAC = MAC₄₀ × 10^(−0.00269 × (age − 40)). MAC₄₀ values are agent-specific (sevoflurane 2.05, isoflurane 1.17, desflurane 6.6, halothane 0.75, N₂O 104). The formula is non-linear and validated against the foundational papers by Eger et al. The 70-year-old MAC is approximately 75% of the 40-year-old MAC for volatile agents.
What about local anesthetic safety?
The Local Anesthetic Max Dose calculator enforces both weight-based dosing and absolute caps. Lidocaine: 4.5 mg/kg plain (max 300 mg), 7 mg/kg with epinephrine (max 500 mg). Bupivacaine: 2 mg/kg plain (max 175 mg), 3 mg/kg with epi (max 225 mg). Ropivacaine: 3 mg/kg both routes (max 225 mg). Mepivacaine: 5 mg/kg plain, 7 mg/kg with epi. Cardiotoxicity warnings appear for bupivacaine. Pair with the LAST Lipid Rescue tool for the rescue protocol if toxicity occurs.
Does it work without internet?
Yes, 100% offline. All formulas, drug references, MAC values, LA max-dose tables, and citations are bundled into the app. Nothing is transmitted to any server. No patient data collection, no tracking, no accounts. Critical for OR use where Wi-Fi is unreliable, and for international or field-hospital deployment.
What about sugammadex dosing?
VerMed Anes implements full sugammadex dosing per the Bridion prescribing information: 2 mg/kg for moderate blockade (T2 reappearance), 4 mg/kg for deep blockade (post-tetanic count 1–2), 16 mg/kg for immediate reversal of high-dose rocuronium (e.g., cannot-intubate-cannot-ventilate within ~3 min of induction). Also calculates traditional neostigmine + glycopyrrolate dosing for facilities without sugammadex.

No. VerMed Anes is exempt from FDA medical device regulation under the 21st Century Cures Act §520(o)(1)(E) — it is a clinical reference tool, not a medical device. It does not diagnose, treat, or monitor a specific patient. It provides generalized educational calculations for trained anesthesia clinicians to interpret in the context of the full clinical picture.

What about pediatric dosing?
Pediatric guards are built into the engines. Propofol Infusion Syndrome (PRIS) warnings trigger for age < 3 yr. ET tube sizing uses age-based formulas (Cole's: age/4 + 4 uncuffed; age/4 + 3.5 cuffed for age ≥ 2 yr) with neonatal/preterm tables per APLS. Anaphylaxis epinephrine is weight-capped (0.01 mg/kg up to 0.3 mg for < 30 kg, up to 0.5 mg for ≥ 30 kg) per AAP 2024 guidance.
