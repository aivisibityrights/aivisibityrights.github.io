Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about VerMed Dent

Which areas of dentistry does it cover?
Every major discipline: caries & risk assessment (DMFT/dmft, CAMBRA, plaque indices, fluoride toxicity), periodontal (pocket depth & CAL with AAP/EFP staging, BOP, Miller mobility, Glickman furcation), orthodontics (Angle, overjet/overbite, Bolton, ANB, Little), endodontics (working length, obturation volume, taper), oral surgery (Pell-Gregory, Winter, Pederson), restorative & prosthodontics (crown-root ratio, biologic width, VITA shade), pediatric dentistry (eruption timing, space maintainers, fluoride varnish), and pharmacology & local anaesthesia.
How accurate are the calculations?
Every engine is coded directly from the published literature and validated by an automated test suite against textbook values (e.g. a 70 kg adult on lidocaine with vasoconstrictor → 490 mg / 13 cartridges; Bolton anterior ratio mean 77.2%; ANB 2° → skeletal Class I; Pederson distoangular + Class III + Position C → 10, very difficult). No AI, no approximations — just the formulas, with a non-finite-output safety net on every result.
Does it work without internet?
Yes, 100% offline. All formulas, reference values, and citations are bundled into the app. Nothing is transmitted to any server. No patient-data collection, no tracking, no accounts. Open and use it anywhere — in clinic, in surgery, or in areas with no connectivity.
How does it handle drug dosing safely?
Local-anaesthetic maximums follow Malamed with weight-based and absolute ceilings, the binding limit, and a max-cartridge count plus agent age cautions. Antibiotic prophylaxis follows AHA 2021 and returns "Not indicated" unless a qualifying high-risk cardiac condition is affirmatively selected (clindamycin is no longer recommended). Fluoride tools warn at the probable toxic dose. Every drug result carries a "confirm against a current pharmacopoeia" disclaimer. These are reference aids, not prescriptions.
Who is this app for?
General dentists, orthodontists, periodontists, endodontists, oral surgeons, pediatric dentists, dental hygienists, and dental students. Every calculator has a plain-language Guide with a worked example, so the app is a learning tool as well as a chairside reference.
Is it FDA-cleared?
No. VerMed Dent is a clinical-reference and educational calculator, not a medical device. It does not diagnose, treat, or monitor a specific patient — it provides generalized educational calculations for trained clinicians to interpret in the context of the full clinical picture.
