Common questions about VerAir

What is the best air quality calculator app for environmental engineers?
VerAir is a professional mobile toolkit combining US EPA AQI, Gaussian Plume dispersion modeling with Pasquill-Gifford stability classes, EPA Stack Testing Methods 1-5 with isokinetic sampling, AP-42 emission factors database, and Thai PCD regulatory compliance checking. Can I run Gaussian Plume dispersion modeling on my phone?
Yes. VerAir includes a full Gaussian Plume dispersion model using Pasquill-Gifford dispersion coefficients (A-F stability classes) with Briggs plume rise equations for both buoyancy-dominated and momentum-dominated stack plumes. It computes ground-level concentrations along the centerline and at off-axis receptors, and finds the maximum impact distance.
Does VerAir support EPA Stack Testing Methods 1-5?
Yes. VerAir implements EPA Method 1 (traverse points), Method 2 (pitot velocity with default S-type Cp = 0.84), Method 3 (Orsat O2/CO2), Method 4 (moisture), and Method 5 (PM isokinetic sampling with the 90-110% acceptable ratio gate). All calculations are reproducible for performance test reports and CEMS RATA verification.
